{% include bookmark.html type="h3" heading="Learning Outcomes" %}

At the end of this course, students should be able to:

  • Understand basic principles of data visualization

  • Understand how to evaluate existing visualizations

  • Understand how to design and prototype standard visualization techniques

Assessment of these outcomes will be done by a combination of quizzes, exercises, homework, and projects.

{% include bookmark.html type="h2" heading="Course Requirements" class="page-header" %}

Students must complex a mix of participation and homework assignments, as well as two projects. The following sections provide more details on each.

{% include bookmark.html type="h3" heading="Participation Assignments" %}

Students must participate in discussions and exercises both in and outside of class. Since these assignments are often subjective and have no single "correct" answer, they are graded based on effort. Example participation assignments include:

  • Peer Feedback: Students are expected to provide peer feedback for all homework submissions and project prototypes. Students will be randomly assigned 2 to 3 other submissions to review, and given approximately 1 week following the assignment deadline to complete the feedback.
    approx. 20 min per submission

  • Graded Discussions: There may occasionally be graded discussions posted either on Canvas or Piazza. For example, these discussions may ask students to comment on the strengths or weaknesses of recent visualizations or new techniques, or ask students to post their favorite visualizations in a specific category.
    approx. 20 min per assignment

  • Data Vis Talks: Students are expected to lead a 10 to 15 minute discussion on a topic in data visualization. Students should choose a topic in data visualization, research that topic, give a brief 5 minute presentation on that topic in class, and then lead the class in a brief 5 minute discussion on that topic. Valid topics include any tools, techniques, papers, or examples related to the topics covered in-class in the previous weeks. The topic must be approved by the instructor.
    approx. 2 hours total (includes prep and talk)

    Students will earn extra credit if they choose to present their own homework submission, and then lead a discussion evaluating their approach.

  • Project Videos: Students are expected to record 5 to 10 minute videos demonstrating their projects. These videos will be shown in class, and students will be asked to vote on their favorites. There are several free software options students can use to record these videos.
    approx. 2 hours total (includes prep and recording)

Students must provide professional, substantive, and constructive comments to receive full credit for participation assignments. The university's policies on student conduct also apply.

{% include bookmark.html type="h3" heading="Homework Assignments" %}

There will be approximately 5 programming homework assignments throughout the semester. These assignments must be completed individually. Grading will be based on functionality—not aesthetics. Each homework will have an associated list of core features and extra features. Students must successfully implement all of the core features to earn a "C" letter grade on the assignment. Once the core features are completed, students may complete additional extra features to earn a "B" or higher letter grade.
approx. 6 to 10 hours per assignment

Students may earn extra credit by implementing more extra features than necessary. However, each homework grade will be capped at 110%.

{% include bookmark.html type="h3" heading="Midterm Project" %}

The midterm project is a team project where teams must create at least 3 to 4 complementary visualizations of a single dataset. The dataset will be provided by the professor. Teams must record a video demonstrating their project, which will be shown in-class after spring break.
approx. 12 to 20 hours total (2 weeks of work)

{% include bookmark.html type="h3" heading="Final Project" %}

The final project is an individual project where students must choose a dataset and create at least 3 to 4 complementary visualizations of that dataset. The dataset must be approved by the instructor. Students will be asked to produce a sketch and prototype of their final projects for evaluation by their peers, and must record a video demonstrating their project to be shown in-class during finals week.
approx. 18 to 30 hours total (3 weeks of work)
